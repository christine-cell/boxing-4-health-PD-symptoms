This activity stimulates force and power in the upper limbs, making it particularly beneficial for clients who struggle with slow, limited movements or tremors. The exercise incorporates a balance component during the throw and requires control and coordination to catch the ball.
ü•ä Key Cue: Encourage big, powerful throws ‚Äî not gentle tosses. The force generation is the therapeutic goal. Don't wait for the ball to come to you; reach and react!

The emphasis is on the power squat ‚Äî enhancing leg and lower back strength while promoting full extension and amplitude in the arms and hands. This is a foundational PD Warrior exercise.
ü•ä Key Cue: BIG, open hands ‚Äî wrists extended, arms reaching out, chest UP. Think tall and proud. The arms drive the amplitude, the legs drive the power.

Dynamic forceful movements ‚Äî especially effective for bradykinesia and reduced arm swing.

‚ñº
Centers around arm swing and dynamic, forceful movements. As clients advance, the complexity can be heightened to further challenge their capabilities.
ü•ä Key Cue: Do NOT wait for the scarf to fall ‚Äî and do not try to catch it underarm. The point is to be fast enough to catch it overhead. Speed and reaction are the goal.

Trunk Rotation & Flexibility

‚ñº
Focuses on trunk rotation and BIG, powerful arm and shoulder movements. Contributes to overall flexibility of the back, trunk, and shoulder joints. ü•ä Key Cue: "Embody strength and empowerment" as you rise. This is not a gentle stretch ‚Äî it's a powerful, intentional movement. Look up and reach tall on the return.

The primary goal is to enhance trunk rotation. Many people with Parkinson's experience stiffness and reduced trunk rotation ‚Äî which affects walking, turning, and overall balance. ü•ä Key Cue: Keep eyes focused on your hands throughout the entire movement. The visual tracking adds a coordination and dual-task component to the rotation.

Highly beneficial for synchronizing BIG strides with robust arm swings. Also contributes to 180-degree turns. Requires a clearly marked line on the floor. Knees should elevate significantly during the movement; arms should exhibit forceful, straight swinging motions at the final position.

Weight Shifting & Stepping

Weight shifting + stepping practice. Excellent for minor freezing episodes + glutes.
‚ñ∂ Watch Video
‚ñº
Emphasizes weight shifting and is an effective method for practicing stepping. Particularly valuable when dealing with minor episodes of freezing while walking. Also provides an excellent workout for the gluteal muscles!
ü•ä Key Cue: Exaggerate the weight shift side to side ‚Äî make it dramatic and deliberate. This overlearning of the weight-shift pattern is what makes it effective for real-life walking improvement.
‚ö†Ô∏è Precautions

Box Step
Counteract stooped posture ‚Äî strengthens flexors and extensors, elongates the body.
‚ñ∂ Watch Video
‚ñº
Why focus on posture? We work with clients to counteract the stooped posture that develops with aging and Parkinson's. Posture exercises strengthen the front (flexors) and back muscles (extensors). The body follows the arms ‚Äî reaching in different directions helps create a good stretch and elongates the body for improved posture.
ü•ä Key Cue: "The body follows the arms" ‚Äî where the arms go, the spine follows. Use tall, directional reaches to actively pull the client out of their stooped pattern and into an upright, open position.
‚ö†Ô∏è Precautions
