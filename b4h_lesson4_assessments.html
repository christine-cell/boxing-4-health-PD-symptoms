B4H staff assign clients to the appropriate class ‚Äî not the client themselves. Even if a client uses a cane but believes they belong in a stronger class, the final decision is always yours. Discuss placement at the end of the assessment.

03 ‚Äî Assessment Tests

Tap each test to see tips and what to look for. These tests give you objective, measurable data you can use to track progress over time.

01
Walking Video
Video the client walking the length of the gym and back. ‚òÖ Before & After Gold Standard ‚Äî this footage becomes one of your most powerful motivation tools.
Record from the side AND behind if possible. Look for arm swing, stride length, foot clearance, posture, and gait pattern. Share improvement videos with clients ‚Äî seeing themselves walk better is incredibly motivating.
02
Timed Up & Go (TUG)
Client stands from a chair, walks 10 feet, turns, and returns to sitting. One of the most widely used functional mobility tests in PD research.
Normal for healthy older adults is under 12 seconds. Over 20 seconds indicates high fall risk. Record exact time to the decimal ‚Äî this is a key benchmark to repeat every 8‚Äì12 weeks to show progress.
03
Sit to Stand Test
How many times can the client stand from a chair and sit back down in 30 seconds? Measures lower body strength and functional power.
Watch for use of armrests, asymmetry between sides, and how much effort it takes. Improvement in this test directly correlates with fall prevention and daily independence.
04
Timed Down & Up
How long does it take the client to get down to the ground and back up safely? Critical for fall recovery confidence.
This is about safety and confidence as much as speed. Only perform this test if the environment is safe and the client feels comfortable. Always have a spotter and a chair nearby. Getting comfortable with the floor is a key program goal.
05
Timed Backwards Walk
Client walks backwards 10 feet, timed. Tests spatial awareness, balance, and postural control ‚Äî all key areas affected by PD.
Always have a spotter directly behind the client for this test. Watch for hesitation, foot clearance, and posture. Many clients find this test surprisingly challenging ‚Äî reassure them it's normal and celebrate any improvement.

Safety First ‚Äî Always: If you are not comfortable performing any test due to safety concerns or the client's lack of stability, do not perform it. Your judgment is the final word. Never push a client through a test that feels unsafe.

04 ‚Äî Red Flags to Watch For

These are conditions that require extra care, modifications, or communication with the client's healthcare team. Tap each flag to see how to respond.

ü©∫
High or Low Blood Pressure
‚ñº
Blood pressure issues are very common in PD ‚Äî both high and low. High BP should be monitored by their doctor. We have seen many students with high BP who commit to the program and lower their BP over time ‚Äî which can then cause low BP symptoms like dizziness and lightheadedness during class.
ü•ä Action: If a client experiences dizziness or lightheadedness, tell them to see their doctor ‚Äî their medication may need adjusting now that their BP is improving. This is actually a positive sign!
üíä
Blood Thinners
‚ñº
Clients on blood thinners bruise very easily ‚Äî even light contact can cause significant bruising. This is a medication side effect, not an injury, but it needs to be managed carefully.
ü•ä Action: Assign the softest punching bags in the gym to this client. Monitor regularly for bruising changes. Ensure they're wearing proper hand wraps and gloves.
ü¶¥
Osteoporosis
‚ñº
Osteoporosis significantly increases fracture risk from high-impact activities. Bone density is already reduced and any jarring movement can cause injury.
ü•ä Action: Avoid running, jumping, or jerky movements. Focus on controlled, smooth movements. All impact-based exercises must be modified or eliminated for this client.
üß†
Cognitive Impairment
‚ñº
If a client has difficulty following instructions or processing cognitive tasks during the assessment, they likely need a different class ‚Äî even if they are physically high-functioning. Cognitive capacity affects the ability to keep up and stay safe.
ü•ä Action: Place in a PD ¬æ class where the pace and instruction style is better suited. Having a physically capable client in the wrong class creates frustration and safety risks for everyone.
ü©π
Pre-Existing Injuries & Surgeries
‚ñº
Clients often leave injuries off their health history forms ‚Äî either forgetting, downplaying, or not thinking they're relevant. Knee replacements, hip surgeries, back injuries, and rotator cuff problems all significantly affect what exercises are safe.
ü•ä Action: Always verbally ask about knees, hips, and back during the assessment even if the forms look clear. Probe gently ‚Äî "Any old injuries you've had over the years?" often reveals important information.
