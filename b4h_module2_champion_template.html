Boxing Combinations

Champion boxing uses a slightly different combination structure ‚Äî varied rhythm, different patterns, and built-in recovery intervals. Adapt timing and rounds based on your group.

1
Jab ¬∑ Cross ¬∑ Jab ¬∑ Cross + Switch Stance
Double the basics ‚Äî then a stance switch. Gets both sides working from the start.
2
Jab ¬∑ Cross ¬∑ Big Backstep ¬∑ Knee
The backstep adds footwork and spatial awareness before the knee ‚Äî a big balance and coordination challenge.
3
Jab ¬∑ Cross ¬∑ Cross ¬∑ Jab
Reversed rhythm ‚Äî ends with the jab instead of the cross. Cognitive challenge in the familiar combination pattern.
4
Body ¬∑ Body ¬∑ Head ¬∑ Head
Level changes ‚Äî punch low then high. Requires trunk flexion and extension alternating with each combination.
5
10 Seconds On ¬∑ 10 Seconds Off ¬∑ √ó 3
Interval burst ‚Äî maximum effort for 10 seconds, full rest for 10 seconds, repeat 3 times. Short enough to push hard, long enough to recover. Great for clients managing fatigue.
6
Big Boxing Freestyle
Open round ‚Äî clients choose their own combinations. Freedom within the framework. Their choice, their confidence, their ownership of the movement.
ü•ä Instructor's Discretion: Feel free to change and advance combinations with groups that are more comfortable. The combinations above are starting points ‚Äî read your room and challenge clients appropriately.
