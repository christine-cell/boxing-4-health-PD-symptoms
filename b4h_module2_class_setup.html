üí° The Signature Before & After Trick

One of the most powerful ways to show clients that exercise is working in real time: have them sign their name ‚Äî or write a motivational quote ‚Äî before class, then have them write it again immediately after. In most cases, the post-class handwriting will be noticeably more fluid and controlled.

Why it works: The warm-up and intensity of a B4H class temporarily improves dopamine availability and motor control ‚Äî effects that are visible in handwriting. Clients are often moved by seeing this with their own eyes. It's one of the most tangible "this is working" moments you can give them.

During Class ‚Äî Your Role

You're not just running through exercises ‚Äî you're coaching, watching, adjusting, and leading. Here's what that looks like in practice.

‚úì
Demonstrate Everything
Always demo every exercise and routine before clients attempt it. For PD and senior clients, visual demonstration is far more effective than verbal description alone. Show it big, show it clearly, show modifications at the same time.
Every Class
‚úì
Watch & Correct Technique
Keep your eyes moving around the room constantly. Watch clients during exercises and provide real-time feedback on technique ‚Äî both verbally and through demonstration. Correct technique minimizes injury and maximizes therapeutic benefit. A client doing a movement incorrectly isn't getting the benefit you designed the class to deliver.
Safety
‚úì
Show Modifications for All Levels
When explaining a circuit, always demonstrate at least two levels ‚Äî the standard version and at least one modification for clients with lower fitness, balance concerns, or limited range of motion. No one should ever feel left behind or unsafe because they couldn't keep up with the base version.
Every Class
‚úì
Monitor Progress & Adapt
Watch how clients perform over multiple sessions. Adapt programs as clients improve or as their condition changes. A client who needed a chair last month might not need it today. One who was managing well might have a harder day today. Stay observant and stay flexible.
Ongoing
‚úì
Enforce Safety Rules & Equipment Use
Explain safety rules clearly at the start of every class ‚Äî especially with new clients. Proper technique, equipment handling, spacing between students, and what to do if someone feels unwell. Safety is not a one-time orientation ‚Äî reinforce it consistently.

Handling Money: Instructors are not expected to handle student payments. If money is left with you for any reason, store it securely or take it to the office immediately. Always notify the Head Instructor or Gym Manager. Never hold onto it longer than necessary.

‚ö†Ô∏è Incident Reports

If anything happens in your class ‚Äî a fall, an injury, a medical event, anything unexpected ‚Äî you are required to fill out an Incident Report form immediately.

1
Attend to the client ‚Äî ensure they are safe and stable before anything else.
2
Fill out an Incident Report form. Forms are located in the RED Communication Book in your gym. Complete it thoroughly and accurately while details are fresh.
3
Report to your Head Instructor or Gym Manager immediately ‚Äî don't wait until your next shift or the next day. Contact them as soon as the situation is under control.
4
Document everything. Time, what happened, who was present, what action was taken. The more detail the better.
