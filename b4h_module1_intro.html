Parkinson's Disease Defined

Parkinson's Disease (PD) is a progressive neurological disorder that affects the nervous system and the parts of the body controlled by nerves. It occurs when dopamine-producing neurons in the brain are damaged or die. Because dopamine plays a critical role in coordinating smooth, purposeful movement, its loss results in the movement-related symptoms we associate with the disease.

02 ‚Äî Parkinson's Plus Conditions

Some clients may have a Parkinson's Plus diagnosis ‚Äî conditions involving multiple system degeneration that share symptoms with PD but come with additional complexities. Tap each condition to learn more.

MSA Multiple System Atrophy
‚ñº
Multiple System Atrophy (MSA) is a rare neurodegenerative disorder characterized by autonomic dysfunction (affecting blood pressure, bladder, and digestion), tremors, slow movement, muscle rigidity, and postural instability ‚Äî collectively known as parkinsonism. MSA also involves ataxia (impaired coordination and balance stemming from cerebellar dysfunction). Clients with MSA may fatigue more rapidly and have greater difficulty with blood pressure regulation during exercise.
CBD Corticobasal Degeneration
‚ñº
Corticobasal Degeneration (CBD) is a progressive neurological disorder involving nerve cell loss and atrophy across multiple brain areas, including the cerebral cortex and the basal ganglia. Alongside movement difficulties, clients may experience apraxia (inability to perform purposeful movements), cortical sensory loss, and cognitive changes. Asymmetric limb involvement is a hallmark ‚Äî one side of the body is often more affected.
PSP Progressive Supranuclear Palsy
‚ñº
Progressive Supranuclear Palsy (PSP) primarily affects balance, walking, and eye movement. Clients often experience frequent unexplained falls backward and difficulty moving their eyes vertically ‚Äî an important safety consideration in a boxing environment. Speech and swallowing difficulties are also common.
